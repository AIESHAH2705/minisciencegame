1. Scientific Skills

Scientific skills are abilities that help us explore and understand the world around us. They include observing carefully, asking questions, performing experiments, recording results, and thinking logically. Scientists use these skills to discover new things, solve problems, and explain how things work.

2. Science Room Rules

Science room rules are important for safety and learning. They include wearing protective gear like lab coats and goggles, handling equipment properly, following teacher instructions, keeping the workspace clean, and respecting others. Following these rules prevents accidents and ensures experiments go smoothly.

3. Living and Non-Living Things

Living things grow, move, reproduce, and respond to their environment, such as humans, animals, and plants. Non-living things do not grow, reproduce, or respond to the environment, like rocks, water, and chairs. Learning to differentiate them helps us understand life on Earth.

4. Humans

Humans are living beings with complex bodies, capable of thinking, communicating, and adapting to their surroundings. Learning about humans includes studying body parts, senses, health, and how to maintain a healthy lifestyle through exercise, proper nutrition, and hygiene.

5. Animals

Animals are living organisms that eat food, move, breathe, and reproduce. They can live on land, in water, or in the air. Animals have different structures like legs, wings, or fins to help them survive. Some are pets, some are wild, and some help humans in various ways.

6. Plants

Plants are living organisms that grow in soil, water, or air. They produce their own food through photosynthesis, provide oxygen, and support life on Earth. Plants include trees, flowers, grasses, and shrubs. Understanding plant parts like roots, stems, leaves, flowers, and fruits is essential for learning about nature.

7. Magnet

Magnets are objects that attract certain metals like iron and steel. They have north and south poles and are used in compasses, electronics, and tools. Studying magnets helps us learn about forces and how objects can move without being touched.

8. Absorption

Absorption is the process where one substance soaks up or takes in another. For example, a sponge absorbs water. Plants absorb water and nutrients through roots. Absorption is important in everyday life and is essential for living things to survive.

9. Earth

Earth is the planet we live on. It has land, water, air, and living organisms. Learning about Earth includes studying continents, oceans, weather, natural resources, and environmental care. Understanding our planet helps us protect it for the future.

10. Basic Structures

Basic structures are the shapes and arrangements of parts in living and non-living things. For example, bones provide support to the human body, stems support plants, and buildings have beams and walls. Understanding structures helps us know how things work and stay strong.
